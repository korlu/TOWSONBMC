Solution:

                The two solutions to such problem are to (1) Live with it, or (2) Get rid off it. We propose the second solution as the first one is already in place.  In order to get rid off the OS, we must learn new and radical ways of doing computing.  In order to eliminate OS, we need to eliminate four basic functions of OS; sharing resources (memory, CPU, I/O), multiplexing resources, provide “abstractions to hardware, and provide “application knowledge in OS (program loading and exit, error and recovery, tools, and other interfaces).”  In our approach, we eliminate sharing of resources, and disperse the “abstractions to hardware” and “application knowledge in OS” into applications, thus constituting a dispersed operating system computing (DOSC) paradigm.  When sharing of resources is eliminated, multiplexing of resources is not needed.  We are currently pursuing such paradigm and demonstrated the feasibility by illustrating sample applications such as Web server, email and VoIP softphone that run on a bare machine without any OS. We have made significant progress in this endeavor and hopefully convince the world to go with it!
