Course Objectives

 

The primary objective of this course is to prepare students to understand complex database management system issues and acquire knowledge with respect to current research and product directions.  After this course, students will be ready to face a challenging database assignment in industry, or they will be equipped with the knowledge and background to conduct research in database systems.  Some of the database topics to be studied in this course include: middleware and data integrators, transaction management, query processing, distributed DBMSs, object-oriented DBMSs, object-relational DBMSs, and Emerging Database Technologies.

 

Projects

 

There is a mandatory group project based on Middleware and Data Integrators.  Four to maximum of six students can form a group and design, implement, and demonstrate a middleware project. The study will enable students to learn modern approaches to middleware and data integrator problems and solutions, and provide hands on experience in SQL and ODBC/JDBC.

 

There are three graded parts to the project.  Do not forget that 50% of the grade is based on the project! The three parts of the grade for the project is based on your initial proposal; design, implementation, demonstration; and presentation, and report.  Working project is very crucial and carries more weight as students get first hand experience on implementation.   

 

During the project discussions, numerous project ideas and details will be described in the class.  You will be given a specific project topic during this semester, which involves four library databases to be connected with a middleware of your choice. The middleware construction may require Java, C/C++, or XML programming language. If you do not have one of the above programming language background, you may not take this course. Partner selection will be done on a random assignment in the class. Once a group selection is made, you cant change from one group to another.  Once you have selected a topic for the project, write small proposal 2-4 pages including the party members, problem description, design and implementation plan, and expected outcome.  This report must be submitted before end of 3rd week and get it approved by the faculty.  No partners should be changed after initial assignment of groups.

 

After the presentation, the project report should be complete and submitted to the instructor.  A soft copy in PPT Presentation is preferred, along with hardcopy of the report.   The report should contain design, implementation, demonstration details, and final outcome.  All the code and data files should be submitted on a disk along with appropriate instructions to use the code and run your system. Each group will present their project in the last week of classes; they will be given 30 minutes for their presentation. In addition, each group should present their working project in the lab to the faculty.  A single presenter will be randomly chosen to present your project in the class.  All the group members must be present during the project presentations.      

 

If one or more partners of your group complain about participation of a given student in the group, he/she will get zero points in the project.  Make sure that you have shown best efforts and significant contribution in the group project.  During the final presentation in class, you must describe your role in the project and details of your participation.
